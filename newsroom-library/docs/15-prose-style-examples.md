# 15 - Prose Style Examples

## Purpose

This is the gold-standard style sheet for Para League newsroom drafts.

The model should imitate the energy of these examples, not their exact wording. The goal is expressive, player-facing poker prose that still obeys one small safety layer: do not invent game facts.

## Core Safety Layer

Do not invent hands, cards, actions, results, quotes, table talk, emotions, rivalries, season outcomes, clinches, or standings movement.

Everything else has room to breathe. The draft can be punchy, funny, dramatic, rhythmic, and a little dangerous when the verified record gives it something to stand on.

## Tone Target

Closer to poker table folklore, league newsroom, player dossier, sharp sports recap, and archive caption.

Not generic ESPN. Not sterile. Not corporate. Not fake anime. Not sportsbook hype.

The table remembers what happened.

## Player Profile

panicmixie's first page is not a victory lap. It is better than that. It is unfinished. Second place, 7 points, 70 hands, and two late 8,000-chip marks give the profile something to stand on. Not a legend yet. Not a footnote either.

The next table decides whether this is a pattern or just the first scratch in the glass.

## Winner Profile

Para-Poker gets the cleanest first sentence in the S0 file: first session, first place, 10 points, biggest pot on the board. That does not crown anything yet, but it does give everyone else a name to chase.

Hand #6 is the center of the page. A 15,290-chip pot, a two-pair finish, and the kind of early swing that makes the result feel earned.

## Runner-Up Profile

panicmixie did not win S0-001, but the record does not get to ignore them. Second place brought 7 points, and the late 8,000-chip pots kept their name in the session after the biggest swing went the other way.

That is a useful kind of unfinished. The first result says runner-up. The next result gets to decide whether the chase has teeth.

## Moment Blurb

Hand #6 is the first dent in the season. A 15,290-chip pot, a two-pair finish, and the kind of early swing that makes the rest of S0-001 read differently.

## Standings Blurb

The board is barely dry, but it is no longer blank. Para-Poker owns the first line. panicmixie owns the first chase. That is not a season story yet, but it is enough to make the next table matter.

## Article Lead

Season 0 does not have history yet. It has a first mark. Para-Poker put it there in S0-001, turning the opener on Hand #6 and leaving the standings with a clean name at the top.

The chase starts immediately. panicmixie left with 7 points, enough late resistance to keep the result from reading flat, and the first job every runner-up gets: make the next page louder.

## Social Or Card Copy

Hand #6 kicked open S0-001. Para-Poker took 15,290 chips, 10 points, and the first line on the board. panicmixie took the chase. The season has a pulse.

## Writer Notes

Fragments are allowed. Rhythm is allowed. Attitude is allowed. Make winners feel cool and non-winners feel worthy when the facts support it.

Bold is not the problem. Lying is the problem.
