# 14 - League Article Guide

## Purpose

Generate broader Para League articles from an editorial request and structured league data.

## Shape

- Hook: why does this article exist now?
- Main thread: the central league story.
- Supporting evidence: standings, sessions, moments, or players supplied.
- Boundary: what the article cannot claim yet.

## Article Types

- Beat report: current league coverage from the newest verified development.
- Feature angle: a focused public article around one supplied player, session, standings thread, or moment pattern.
- Preseason context: early-season framing that treats results as opening markers, not conclusions.
- Player race: current standings/player comparison using only supplied ranks, points, and approved data.
- Weekly digest: compact coverage of current approved beats. If data is limited, call it a short current update.
- Preview setup: upcoming-session or watch-list setup without predictions or invented stakes.

## Season Lifecycle Rules

- Always respect the supplied `seasonStatus`.
- If `seasonStatus` is `in_progress`, write as if the league story is still being formed.
- Do not describe standings snapshots as final outcomes.
- Do not imply a race has ended, a player has clinched, or a season has resolved unless the structured data explicitly says so.
- Treat Season 0 preseason data as current league evidence, not completed history.

## Ongoing-Season Language

Use language that keeps the board alive:

- current
- so far
- through the available sessions
- early board
- opening marker
- current standings line
- for now
- still forming

## Forbidden Finality Language

Avoid these phrases unless supplied data explicitly says the season is complete:

- ended the season
- closed the campaign
- final standings
- secured the season
- clinched
- locked up
- completed the race
- season-long dominance
- proved once and for all

## Approved Phrases

- the current board says
- through the available sessions
- the early line belongs to
- the table has started to take shape
- for now
- the first marker is down
- the race is still forming
- the sample is still young
- this is not a conclusion yet
- the next session can change the read

## Do

- Ask the supplied data to carry the story.
- Keep the tone public, clear, and league-aware.
- Put missing information in admin notes, not the article body.
- Use ongoing-season language when the season is still active.
- Keep current standings framed as markers, not final verdicts.

## Do Not

- Invent news, quotes, drama, rivalry, or future events.
- Pad with generic sports phrases.
- Publish a data summary disguised as an article.
- Write as if the season, preseason, standings race, or player story is complete unless the supplied season status is complete.
