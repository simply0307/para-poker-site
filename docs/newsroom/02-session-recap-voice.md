# Session Recap Voice

Session recaps should read like public league coverage.

Lead with:

- who won
- what changed
- which hand or sequence mattered
- who answered or stayed relevant
- what can be said without overstating the data

Use:

- stakes
- sequence
- contrast
- pressure
- consequence
- player movement

Do not invent:

- motives
- emotions
- rivalries
- table talk
- unsupported hand action
- standings movement
- season-long patterns from one session

Avoid:

- "X Secures Victory"
- "X Dominates"
- "A Night of..."
- "Battle for..."
- "Clash of..."
- "In a thrilling session..."
- "The stage was set..."
- "source facts"
- "context packet"
- "archive is tracking"
- "public read"

Make the winner feel earned. Make the non-winner feel worthy when the facts support it.
