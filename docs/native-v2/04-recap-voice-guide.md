# Para Poker Recap Voice Guide

## Voice Target

Player-facing, clear, grounded, competitive, archive-aware, and lightly mythic.

The tone should feel like public sports coverage that knows the table has history. It can be vivid, but the facts must stay clean.

## Voice Hierarchy

1. Accuracy.
2. Clarity.
3. Player dignity.
4. Layer-appropriate style.
5. Jokes and mythic flavor.

## Good Examples

> Para-Poker opened S0-001 with the first approved win of the preseason, taking 10 points across a 70-hand session.

> The largest listed pot came early. Hand #6 gave Para-Poker a 15,290-chip swing and a moment the archive can actually point to.

> Panicmixie did not leave the night empty. A second-place finish, 7 points, and multiple late-session pots gave the profile a clear starting line.

> The table did not need fake prophecy. It had a result, a pressure hand, and the first standings shape of Season 0.

## Too Cold

> Player has 70 hands, 10 points, rank #1. Source facts are attached.

> Deterministic session artifact generated from player_session_stats and notable_hands.

> No sourceFacts found for this scope.

Why it fails: sounds internal, mechanical, and unworthy of a public player profile.

## Too Corny

> Para-Poker summoned destiny and became the eternal king of the felt.

> The river opened a sacred gate and crowned the chosen one of the preseason.

> Panicmixie was destroyed in a brutal collapse.

Why it fails: invents stakes, overuses lore, or humiliates a player.

## Allowed Creative Liberties

Writers and future generation systems may:

- describe pressure when a hand is tagged all-in, large pot, showdown, or standings-relevant
- call a verified large pot a "swing" or "turning point candidate"
- say a session "entered the archive" when it has a session record
- use phrases like "the table remembered" as tone flavor
- summarize patterns only when supported by repeated data
- use "appears", "listed", or "currently" when data confidence is limited
- frame a player as building a season identity from public stats and moments

## Forbidden Claims

Do not claim:

- a player was afraid, tilted, choking, reckless, cowardly, angry, or desperate
- a bluff happened unless the data proves it
- a player intended something unless a quote or action record supports it
- a rivalry exists from one isolated hand
- a player secured a reward unless confirmed
- a hand changed standings unless standings impact is known
- private quotes, images, or identity details without opt-in
- cash stakes, buy-ins, rake, or betting outcomes
- major Paralleon/Reath canon from a poker session

## Dramatic But Fair

Good:

> A late lead became vulnerable when the pot pressure shifted, but the recap should credit the player who created the swing.

Avoid:

> Tony choked like a loser.

Principle: recaps may describe pressure, mistakes, swings, and painful endings, but they must not shame players or erase the winner.

## Public Copy Checklist

- Does every factual claim come from data or approved editorial fields?
- Would the player recognize this as fair?
- Could a new visitor understand it without league lore?
- Is the mythic flavor optional rather than required?
- Did we avoid raw IDs, debug labels, and database language?
